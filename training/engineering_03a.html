Engineering Orientation 03a

Power Distribution Introduction

Modern starships are powered primarily by antimater reactors often refered to as warp cores. Power from the core is shunted to a special warp plasma relay that redirects it to various systems. Many of the ships primary systems connect directly to the warp relay, such as the nacelles, phaser banks, and structural integrity field. Secondary systems, which consume significantly less power, receive their power from smaller plasma distribution grids (EPS grids). These grids in turn get their plasma from the main warp relay.

In addition to the warp core, a starship will typically have two other sources of power: Impulse fusion reactors, and battery backups. Impulse reactors generate the charge and plasma required for sub-light impulse flight, but can also be used as alternate power sources for EPS grids, meaning that if warp power is offline, the engineer may opt to attach an EPS grid instead to the impulse reactors. If both impulse and warp systems are offline, emergency battery backups may be used, to maintain life support and other critical systems.

It should be noted that the different power sources aboard a starship each generate orders of magnitude of different power output. A warp core is required for powering a ship's nacelles, and without it you will be incapable of warp flight. Similarily, powering phasers off an impulse reactor would significantly increase the time required to charge their reserves.
