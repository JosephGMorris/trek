Science Orientation 05

Environmental Science

External environmental factors are of immediate concern for a number of ship's systems, and your fellow bridge officers require up to date information to make correct and timely decisions.

The local particle density restricts maximum warp value, as the navigational deflectors can only safely operate at certain tolerances. Also, local gravitational and subspace distortions will affect warp systems directly, and the engineering and navigation teams will need to be made aware.

Ambient radiation readings are critical for the safety of the crew, and the bridge crew will need to know when the level has spiked so that additional power may be directed to the shields. (In the event that the ambient radiation has overpowered the shields, you will likely also get an internal sensor alert to indicate as much.)
