Engineering Orientation 03b

Power Distribution Safety

As an engineer, you have the ability to increase or decrease the power output of your various reactor sources. There may be many circumstances in your missions where is neccessary to completely cut power, or to immediately reduce the output of a power source. Very rarely, if ever, will you find yourself in a position where you need to directly increase the output of a reactor. There are safeties in place to warn you against doing so.

When a system requires more power, it is best to locate the distribution relay that the system is attached to (the warp realy, or one of the Port, Starboard, Aft, or Forward EPS grids), and to increase the power directly on the system. The ship's computer will calculate the required power distribution of the relay, and make the distribution change, before automatically dialing up the power of the origin reactor.

As an example, if you find that your port shield is draining faster than it can maintain it's charge, say from enemy fire or ambient radiation, you will want to increase the power to that system. Since the port sheild is connected to the port EPS grid, which is in turn connected to the primary relay, which draws power from the warp core, you would have to in turn adjust the distribution and output settings on each of these systems before you would achieve the desired output on the port sheild. What's worse, if you do not correctly time the distribution and output adjustments, you will likely overload several other systems in the process.

By far, the safest method of increasing power to the port shields is simply to select a higher level on it's power consumption bar.
